Game development - Python

This is also an interesting one to talk about. In my first year in college, we had an elective project for a particular subject. The choice had to have something to do with what we had seen that year. Since I was very interested in game development, and we had seen python that year, I thought, I'm going to try to make a 2D game in python.


This was such a project that I started without too many expectations. After a lot of research and long thinking, I finally came up with a theme of sorts. The intention was very simple. You are a witch, and you can shoot arrows at falling aliens with your staff. Your score goes up when you hit an alien, and the game is over when you are touched by the alien.


I think this game improved my python skills super hard. Funny enough, I had a re-exam for python that year, so this was good preparation for my re-exam. (Yes, I passed my re-exam, perhaps thanks to this experience)

RCS challenge

I don't have much to say about this, but I thought it was quite an accomplishment. In my first year of college, I participated with a team in a 24h Cybersecurity Challenge.


In the first year we only had general subjects, so this was a fun challenge to see what cybersecurity has to offer. There were 5 different categories to "hack" with 5 levels of difficulty each time. The goal was to find as many exploits as possible from these 25 challenges within 24 hours.


There were about 3000 participants and we finished 272. It was fairly difficult, but a super fun experience!
